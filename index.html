The 3 pain scenarios

"I found out three weeks late."

A game in your genre ran a 40% discount for a week and picked up a wave of new reviews. You didn't notice until you scrolled past it on the store front page. By then the moment to react was gone.

"I don't have an hour a day for this."

SteamDB and Gamalytic are great, if you actually open them. Most solo devs don't check them daily — so the monitoring you're supposed to be doing quietly stops after week one.

"I need a tap on the shoulder, not a dashboard."

Spreadsheet, bookmark folder, calendar reminder to "check competitors" — works for ten days, then it doesn't. What you actually want: nothing, nothing, nothing, then one message that says "this happened, go look."

What we monitor

  • Price changes and discounts — including regional pricing, not just US price
  • Review count spikes — a sudden jump usually means a launch, a big sale, or a wave of press/streamers
  • Chart movement — entering or dropping out of Top Sellers / New & Trending / Popular Upcoming
  • Major updates — a big patch, DLC announcement, or store page overhaul

Threshold sensitivity is adjustable per game — you decide what counts as "worth a ping" vs. noise.

FAQ

Where does your data come from?

Everything we track is built on Steam's own public store and API data — the same publicly visible prices, review counts, charts, and store pages anyone can see by visiting the Steam store. We don't scrape anything private, and we don't need your Steam account or credentials to track a competitor's public game page.

Are you affiliated with Valve or Steam?

No. We are an independent, third-party tool built by an indie developer, for indie developers. "Steam" refers to Valve's platform; we are not Valve, and this product is not endorsed by or affiliated with Valve Corporation.

Can I cancel anytime?

Yes. The standard monthly plan cancels anytime, no questions asked. Founding annual members are covered by a straightforward refund policy if we don't ship what we promised — details go out with your receipt.

How often will I actually get alerted?

That depends entirely on your competitors — that's the point. If nothing crosses your threshold, you get zero emails that week. This is deliberately not a daily digest. Expect anywhere from a few alerts a month to a few a week, depending on how active your tracked list is.

What alert channels do you support?

Email at launch, plus a Discord webhook so alerts can drop straight into your studio's server. If you need Slack or something else, tell us on the waitlist form — we're prioritizing based on what people actually ask for.
